Quick Reference

Where Things Go

A simple map of where files land on your Mac so you always know where to look.

📸 Screenshots ● Auto-sorted
~/Pictures/Screenshots/

Every screenshot you take (Cmd+Shift+3 or Cmd+Shift+4) saves here, then files itself into a folder by month, automatically, within seconds.

In Finder: Pictures › Screenshots › pick the month

  • 2026-04
  • 2026-05
  • 2026-06
  • …new months appear on their own
⬇️ Downloads ● Tidy on request
~/Downloads/

Anything you save from the web, get from Claude, or receive as an email attachment lands here first. It's the catch-all inbox.

In Finder: Downloads (in the sidebar)

It's sorted into type-folders so older files are easy to find. New downloads sit loose at the top until you ask for a tidy-up.

  • PDFs
  • Word Docs
  • Images
  • Spreadsheets
  • Presentations
  • Installers
  • Videos & Audio
  • Calendar & Data
  • Web & Misc
🖥️ Desktop
~/Desktop/

Kept clean, just active project folders, nothing loose. No more screenshot pile-up on the background.

In Finder: Desktop (in the sidebar)

📄 Documents (on this Mac)
~/Documents/

Your local documents, sorted into type-folders. This is one of the two "Documents" you see in the Finder sidebar.

In Finder: Documents (the one stored on this Mac)

☁️ OneDrive (cloud)
~/Library/CloudStorage/OneDrive-Personal/

Your cloud files, backed up and synced across devices. Already neatly organized into numbered folders, this is the second "Documents" in your sidebar.

In Finder: OneDrive (in the sidebar)

  • 01_MedSchool & MCAT
  • 02_Personal, School, Family
  • 03_Admin & Legal
  • 04_Career & Applications
  • 05_Vehicles & Insurance
  • 06_Finances
  • 10_CHOP
The one rule to remember: screenshots go to Pictures › Screenshots (and sort themselves). Everything else you download goes to Downloads. That's it.
